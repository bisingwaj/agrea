Since the revision of the OHADA Uniform Act relating to Commercial Companies and Economic Interest Groups (AUSCGIE), the **Simplified Joint Stock Company (SAS)** has become a preferred choice for structuring ambitious projects or attracting investment funds in the DRC.

## SARL vs SAS: Why choose the SAS?

The SARL (Limited Liability Company) has long been the norm. However, the SAS offers undeniable advantages for certain entrepreneur profiles:

1.  **Statutory Freedom**: This is the main asset. Shareholders freely determine the rules for the organization and operation of the company in the bylaws.
2.  **Attractiveness for Investors**: The SAS allows the issuance of different categories of shares (preferred shares), which facilitates fundraising.
3.  **Single President**: A single president can lead the SAS (individual or legal entity), offering great decisional agility.

## The 4 Key Steps to Create a SAS at the GUCE

The Single Window for Business Creation (GUCE) centralizes the procedures.

### 1. Drafting the bylaws
Unlike a SARL where model bylaws may suffice, drafting the bylaws of a SAS requires the expertise of an OHADA law jurist to fully exploit its flexibility.

### 2. Capital deposit
The share capital must be deposited in an approved bank or with a notary (who issues a deposit certificate). There is no minimum capital required by law for a SAS, unless otherwise specified in the bylaws.

### 3. Declaration at the GUCE
Submission of the file including the bylaws, the deposit certificate, and the identity documents of the managers/shareholders.

### 4. Obtaining the RCCM and Nat. Id.
The GUCE issues the Trade and Personal Property Credit Register (RCCM), the National Identification, and the Tax Number.

> **Point of attention**: The appointment of a Statutory Auditor is not mandatory at incorporation, unless the company meets certain conditions of balance sheet, turnover, or personnel.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

### Can a SAS have only one shareholder?
Yes. This is called a SASU (Single-member Simplified Joint Stock Company). The operation remains the same.

### Does it cost more to create a SAS than a SARL?
Administrative fees (GUCE, Notary) are similar. However, the fees for drafting the bylaws may be slightly higher due to the complexity and personalization required.
