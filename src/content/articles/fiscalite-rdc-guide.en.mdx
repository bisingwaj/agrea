The tax environment in the Democratic Republic of Congo (DRC) can seem complex for both new investors and established companies. This detailed guide decipher the main tax obligations that a Limited Liability Company (SARL) must face in 2026.

## Business Profits and Benefits Tax (IBP)

The IBP is the main direct tax for companies in the DRC.

*   **Applicable rate**: 30% of taxable profit.
*   **Filing deadline**: Before March 30 of the year following the accounting period.
*   **Provisional installments**: Payable in four annual installments.

> Keeping rigorous accounting (OHADA accounting system) is essential for the exact determination of taxable profit and to avoid penalties from the tax administration (DGI).

## Value Added Tax (VAT)

VAT is an indirect tax charged on goods and services.

*   **General rate**: 16%.
*   **Exemptions**: Certain basic necessities or agricultural equipment may be exempt (to be checked according to the current finance law).
*   **Filing**: Monthly, no later than the 15th of the month following the operations.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

### Does a small business (SME) pay IBP at the rate of 30%?
Micro-businesses and small businesses may be subject to a simplified flat-rate or synthetic regime, often more advantageous, depending on their turnover.

### What are the penalties for late filing?
Delays result in assessment penalties (usually 20% or 40% in case of recurrence) and late interest of 2% per month of delay.
